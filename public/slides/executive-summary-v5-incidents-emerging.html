Incidents & learning1 Sev 1 · 2 near misses
Commentary
A Sev 1 data breach exposed 12,400 customer records through an API flaw. The regulator has been notified and remediation is being managed through the Data Protection action, including rebuilding and retesting the failed Customer Data Retention Control by 15 May. Closure should be based on sustained effectiveness and ongoing monitoring, not completion of the technical fix alone. Two near misses—a payment batch caught by reconciliation and a customer data export blocked by DLP—showed detective controls operating as intended but exposed earlier process weaknesses. The resulting actions should be visible in the Q2 test plan, with named owners, dates and evidence that preventive controls have been strengthened.

Eight emerging risks are tracked. AI / GenAI Model Risk and Systemic Cyber Event have moved from Monitor to Board Watch and sit within a 0–12 month impact horizon; both are rising fast. Their proximity means governance, approval controls, systemic dependency mapping and credible response scenarios should receive direct Board challenge. Management response should be prioritised by time to impact, with clear early-warning indicators and owners before risks crystallise.
